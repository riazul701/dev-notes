# USB (Universal Serial Bus)

## USB HUB

* Always attch usb extension cable with usb hub and check whole thing works perfectly or not.

* You can daisy-chain multiple hubs for greater reach, but USB specifications limit you to 5 hubs per connection to maintain stability. Multiple extensions increase latency. <sup>{1}</sup>

* All devices share the same USB link bandwidth. USB 2.0: 480 Mbps total. USB 3.0: 5 Gbps total. <sup>ChatGPT: maximum number of usb hub port from a single usb motherboard port</sup>

* Do not buy switch controlled USB HUB, because switch and USB port do not work after some time.

## USB Extension Cable

* Minimum 2 Meter / 6 Feet USB Extension Cable Required

* Recommended (maximum) cable length for USB 3.2 is 3 meter (9 feet) <sup>{1}</sup>

* Stay on table: On a 2 inch PVC pipe, make several (3/4) half width cuttings and attch USB extension cable with rubber.

## DIY USB Dust-Proof Cap

* Safety: Check short circuit protection.

* Buy cheap/damaged usb extension cable.

* Two sides of USB extension cable works as USB cap for both male and female ports, when separate them from cable and use plastic/pvc tape as electric insulator.
  * Separately tape (pvc) positive and negative wires to prevent short circuit.

## Computer Table Safety And Common Concepts

* DO NOT USE IRON/STEEL IN COMPUTER TABLE, BECAUSE THERE ARE MANY COMPUTER RELATED DEVICES ON TABLE, THEREFORE RISK OF ELECTRIC SHOCK. ALSO CPU EMITS SMALL AMOUNT OF ELECTRICITY BECAUSE OF BUILDING'S EARTHING PROBLEM.
  * Use a multimeter to check that the coated pipe is electrically isolated from earth. Check at multiple points to ensure there are no gaps or thin spots.
  * Make MS (Mild Steel) box pipes electrically insulated using gas pipe tape.
    * Do NOT rely on duct tape or electrical tape → melts, tears, poor dielectric strength.
  * Heat Shrink Tube, heat-shrink sleeves, wraparound sleeves
  * Coat the Table with Electrically Insulating Material <sup>ChatGPT: "DIY make iron table electrically insulated"</sup>
    * Useful if you want to keep the metal exposed but insulated.
    * Recommended coatings:
      * Epoxy resin coating (thick, strong dielectric)
      * Polyurethane varnish
      * Powder coating (if you have access)
      * Electrical insulation spray (acrylic or polyurethane conformal coat)
    * Steps:
      * 1. Sand the metal lightly (120–220 grit).
      * 2. Clean with isopropyl alcohol.
      * 3. Apply 2–4 coats.
      * 4. Let fully cure (12–48 hours depending on coating).
    * Important: Thin paint alone is not reliable for real electrical insulation. Epoxy or multiple thick coats are better.
    * Insulate the Table Legs (to isolate from floor and ground). This reduces the chance the table becomes a conductive path to ground.
    * Frame: Optional epoxy coat + grounding

* WHERE 4 PARALLEL 1"X1.5" PIECES ARE CUT AT MIDDLE POSITION, THERE ATTACH TWO 1"X1.5" IRON BAR USING WEILDING, ONE FOR LEFT AND ANOTHER FOR RIGHT SIDE. LEFT AND RIGHT SIDE IRON BAR WILL BE JOINED USING SCREWS.

* FOR UPPER DECK, KEYBOARD-MOUSE TRAY AND LOWER DECK, USE COMBINTATION OF 1"X1.5", 1"X1" AND 1/2"X1/2" MS BOX PIPE TO MAKE TABLE LIGHT, EASILY SUPPORT THIN PLYWOOD AND COST EFFECTIVE.
  * Make separate pillar for lower deck whose height will be 1/2/3 inches. This very short pillars and table main pillars will be joined using screws.

* Use wooden plywood/partex board upto half (portable) / one (non-portable) inch thickness. Therefore iron frame's height will be 29.5 (portable) or 29 (non-portable) inches. Make all measurements using this.
  * Create "group" in upper deck, keyboard-mouse tray and lower deck. On 4 corners there will be 4 "group", which will cover both sides like front-left, front-right, rear-left, rear-right.
  * Use cable tie to attach wooden plywood/partex board to iron frame. For this, make holes in plywood/partex board.

* On left and right side pillar, iron frame and plywood all will be equal and on one/same line. On front side iron frame and plywood will be equal and pillar will be 1 inch inside. On rear side iron frame and plywood will be equal and pillar will be 2 inches inside.

* Screw holes in MS (Mild Steel) box pipe, makes it weaker. Find a workaround of this issue.
