# Concept

> ChatGPT Question: 
> 
> air fryer standard size

Air fryers are usually categorized by **basket capacity (liters)** rather than physical dimensions.

| Capacity    | Suitable For | Typical Basket Size       |
| ----------- | ------------ | ------------------------- |
| **2–3 L**   | 1 person     | Small                     |
| **3.5–4 L** | 1–2 people   | Compact                   |
| **5–6 L**   | 2–4 people   | Standard (most popular)   |
| **6.5–8 L** | 4–6 people   | Large                     |
| **9–12 L**  | 5–8 people   | Extra large or oven-style |

***### Standard size***

For most households, a **5–6 L air fryer** is considered the standard size. It can typically cook:

* 700–1,000 g of fries
* 8–12 chicken wings
* 4–6 chicken drumsticks
* A small whole chicken (around 1–1.3 kg, depending on the model)

***### Typical dimensions***

A 5–6 L basket-style air fryer is usually around:

* **Width:** 28–35 cm (11–14 in)
* **Depth:** 32–40 cm (12.5–16 in)
* **Height:** 30–36 cm (12–14 in)

***### Power consumption***

| Capacity |     Typical Power |
| -------- | ----------------: |
| 2–3 L    |     1,000–1,300 W |
| 4–5 L    |     1,300–1,500 W |
| 5–6 L    | **1,500–1,800 W** |
| 7–8 L    |     1,700–2,000 W |

***### Which size should you buy?***

* **1 person:** 3–4 L
* **2 people:** 4–5 L
* **3–4 people:** **5–6 L (recommended)**
* **5+ people:** 7 L or larger

If you're in Bangladesh and buying for a typical family of **3–4 members**, a **5.5 L or 6 L air fryer** offers a good balance of capacity, countertop space, and energy use.
